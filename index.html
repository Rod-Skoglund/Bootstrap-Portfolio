About Me

My name is Rod Skoglund. I am married and have 4 kids. I have a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering and have been working in Avionics embedded system development for 30 years.

I was born in Phoenix, Arizona. I have lived in Delaware, Oregon, Washington and back to Arizona before settling here in Kansas.

I am a Technical Leader with 23 years leading Application Support and Engineering Development Teams and 8 years in Embedded System Development. Recognized for building high-performing global teams, motivating through significant challenges, and collaborating with other teams to achieve strategic goals. Demonstrated outstanding communication and negotiation skills as demonstrated with upper management, customers, peers, and team members with project management and coordination. Expertise in full life-cycle development using Waterfall, Object-Oriented, and Agile development methodologies. Proven excellence in project, budget and resource management.

I have co-authored a book on Software Engineering ("Program Smarter, Not Harder: Get Mission-Critical Projects Right the First Time" (ISBN 0-07-021232-5) published by McGraw-Hill, Inc) - I did not choose the title. I also co-authored "Code Reuse in Ada and C++", an article published in May 1993 issue of Embedded Systems Programming.
